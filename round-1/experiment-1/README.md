## Full Summary

Implemented and evaluated two methods for detecting near-duplicate short text snippets on a synthetic dataset of 500 text pairs across 5 trials. The baseline method uses character n-gram similarity (n=3) with Jaccard distance thresholding and achieved perfect performance (Precision=1.000, Recall=1.000, F1=1.000). The proposed method uses MinHash LSH (Locality-Sensitive Hashing) with word k-shingles (k=2) and achieved high precision but lower recall (Precision=1.000, Recall=0.885±0.013, F1=0.939±0.007). Threshold tuning was performed to find the optimal MinHash similarity threshold of 0.1. While the baseline method is more accurate for small datasets, MinHash LSH offers better scalability for large-scale applications due to sub-linear query time with LSH indexing.
